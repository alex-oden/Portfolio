01.
The Challenge

A great product hidden behind a broken first impression.

The client's analytics team had been ringing alarm bells for months: nearly 4 out of every 10 trial users churned within their first session. Despite a mature product with strong retention among activated users, the onboarding funnel was leaking at every step.

Marketing was driving qualified traffic. Sales was closing deals. But the moment users hit the product, momentum collapsed. The team had tried surface-level fixes — none of which moved the needle.

I was brought in to lead a deeper investigation: what was actually breaking, and why?

02.
Research

Listening before solving.

I designed a mixed-methods research program to triangulate behavioural data with qualitative insight:

  • 22 user interviews with new and churned trial users across three customer segments
  • Session replay analysis of 200+ first-time onboarding sessions
  • Funnel decomposition to pinpoint exactly where users dropped
  • Competitive teardown of seven leading SaaS onboarding flows

03.
Solution

Three principles, one redesigned flow.

1. Lead with outcomes, not features.

Users were asking "how does this help me hit my goal?" The new flow opens with a single guided task that delivers real value within the first 90 seconds.

2. Hide complexity until it's earned.

The redesign uses a layered disclosure model: only what's needed right now, only when it's relevant.

3. Make progress visible and reversible.

A persistent progress indicator with the ability to backtrack removed the anxiety of feeling stuck.

05.
Reflections

What I'd do differently.

Research synthesis took longer than I budgeted. Next time I'd front-load tagging and synthesis frameworks before the first interview, not after the last one.

Cross-functional rituals beat documentation. The biggest unlock was the weekly "what did we learn this week" meeting with Sales and CS.

Soft launches are worth the friction. The 10% rollout caught two interaction bugs that would have been visible to every new user.
